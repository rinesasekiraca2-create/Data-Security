
Computer security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks.
These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information.
In cryptography, frequency analysis is one of the oldest techniques used to break simple ciphers.
By studying how often letters appear in a language, we can identify patterns that help reveal hidden messages.
The English language has a predictable distribution of letters, with some appearing much more frequently than others.
This property is very useful when attacking substitution ciphers such as the Caesar cipher.
Understanding patterns in text is an important step in modern data security and cryptanalysis.
